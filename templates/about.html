About the Supreme Court

The United States Supreme Court is the highest federal court in the United States. In court cases that have considerable importance on the way that Americans live, the Supreme Court intervenes based on the principles of the Constitution.

Justices are appointed by the President of the United States and confirmed by the U.S. Senate. The Court currently consists of nine justices: one chief justice and eight associate justices. The current composition of the court is: Chief Justice John Roberts, Associate Justice Sonia Sotomayor, Associate Justice Clarence Thomas, Associate Justice Amy Coney Barrett, Associate Justice Brett Kavanaugh, Associate Justice Elena Kagan, Associate Justice Ketanji Brown Jackson, Associate Justice Samuel Alito, and Associate Justice Neil Gorsuch.

The Supreme Court decides on issues of public importance that set a precedence throughout the nation. These issues include gun rights, abortion, affirmative action, voting rights, and many, many more.

About the Website

This website is built using Flask and Python. It consists of a few main components. First and foremost is a discussion forum, where users are able to make posts about topics pertaining to the Supreme Court. Users can also make replies to posts. Users can delete their own posts and comments, and there is additionally support for moderators: users given special access through the backend to delete any posts or comments. This is incredibly important in a forum that is inherently political and thus has a high chance of potential conflict. Users can also view each other's profiles and set their own profile pictures and biographies for other users to read.

Second is a news section. Here, users can look at recent news pertaining to the Court from a diverse array of news sources.

Third is a calendar where users can see upcoming cases and decisions. This is through SCOTUSblog.com.

Lastly is a feedback form where users can submit feedback to the creators of the website (me) regarding suggestions or potential changes.

Much of this website's code is derived from that built by CS50 staff in Problem Set 9 of the course (Finance) as well as my own work from Problem Set 9. This includes the account system (logging in, logging out, user registration, changing password) and much of the CSS and HTML. I give all credit to CS50 staff for building these parts of the code.
